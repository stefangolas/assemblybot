Screw and nut sets or nuts secure brackets and other accessories to rails.

Drop-in fasteners fit directly into the T-slot to attach components—no need to disassemble your existing structure. However, they form less secure connections than end-feed fasteners because less surface area touches the rail.

Spring-loaded ball nuts hold themselves in place, so they won’t slide down vertical rails. The ball glides over holes, so they’re easy to reposition.

Button head screws are more compact, so they're better for tight spaces than other screws for T-slotted framing.

Offset nuts have a mounting hole close to one end, so you can install them next to corners and rail ends.
